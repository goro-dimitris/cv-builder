## General ATS Optimization Guidelines

1. **Formatting:**
   - Use simple, clean formatting (no complex tables or columns)
   - Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
   - Avoid graphics, images, or special characters
   - Use consistent date formats throughout

2. **Keywords:**
   - Research job descriptions for your target roles
   - Include relevant keywords naturally throughout your CV
   - Use industry-standard terminology
   - Spell out abbreviations at least once

3. **Structure:**
   - Use standard section headers (Work Experience, Education, Skills, etc.)
   - Maintain consistent formatting throughout
   - Keep sections in logical order

4. **Content:**
   - Focus on achievements, not just responsibilities
   - Quantify results with numbers and metrics
   - Use action verbs to start bullet points
   - Keep descriptions concise and impactful

5. **File Format:**
   - Save as PDF for best ATS compatibility
   - Ensure the PDF is text-searchable (not just an image)
   - Use a descriptive filename (e.g., "JohnDoe_CV.pdf")

6. **Testing:**
   - Test your CV with ATS tools like Jobscan or Resume Worded
   - Ensure all text is selectable and searchable
   - Check that formatting remains consistent when opened in different PDF viewers
